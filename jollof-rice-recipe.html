Jollof Rice

One-Pot Rice Dish with Tomatoes, Peppers, and Aromatic Spices

Easy 40 min 6 servings Nigerian

Jollof Rice is a beloved West African dish that brings together fragrant rice, rich tomato sauce, and aromatic spices in one pot. This vibrant, flavorful rice dish is a staple at celebrations and family gatherings across Nigeria and beyond.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 3 cups long-grain rice (preferably parboiled)
  • 6 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Tomato Sauce:

  • 6 large tomatoes, blended
  • 2 red bell peppers, blended
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust to taste)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated

For Seasoning:

  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp white pepper
  • 1 stock cube
  • Salt to taste

Optional Additions:

  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, green beans)
  • 1 lb chicken or beef, cut into pieces
  • Fresh herbs for garnish

Instructions

1

Prepare the Rice

Wash the rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Soak in cold water for 30 minutes, then drain completely.

2

Blend the Sauce

In a blender, combine tomatoes, red bell peppers, and scotch bonnet peppers. Blend until smooth and set aside.

3

Cook the Base

Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions and saut√© until translucent. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute.

4

Add the Sauce

Pour in the blended tomato mixture and tomato paste. Cook for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens and oil begins to float on top.

5

Season and Add Rice

Add all the seasonings and stir well. Add the drained rice and stir to coat with the sauce. Pour in hot stock and add bay leaves.

6

Cook to Perfection

Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
