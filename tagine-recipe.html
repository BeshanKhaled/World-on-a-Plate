Tagine

Slow-Cooked Stew with Meat, Vegetables, and Aromatic Spices

Medium 120 min 6 servings Moroccan

Tagine is a traditional Moroccan dish named after the earthenware pot it's cooked in. This slow-cooked stew combines tender meat with vegetables, dried fruits, and a blend of warm spices like cumin, coriander, and cinnamon, creating a rich and aromatic meal.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Meat:

  • 2 lbs (900g) lamb shoulder, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp ginger, grated

For the Spice Blend:

  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Vegetables:

  • 4 carrots, cut into chunks
  • 2 sweet potatoes, cut into chunks
  • 1 cup dried apricots, halved
  • 1 cup dried prunes, pitted
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 2 tbsp honey

For Garnish:

  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint, chopped
  • 1/4 cup almonds, toasted and chopped
  • Couscous or rice for serving

Instructions

1

Prepare the Spice Blend

In a small bowl, combine all the ground spices. Set aside for later use.

2

Brown the Meat

Heat olive oil in a large tagine pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Working in batches, brown the lamb pieces on all sides until golden. Remove and set aside.

3

Saut√© the Aromatics

In the same pot, add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

4

Add Spices and Meat

Stir in the spice blend and cook for 1 minute to release their flavors. Return the browned meat to the pot and toss to coat with spices.

5

Add Liquid and Simmer

Pour in stock and honey, bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 1 hour until meat is tender.

6

Add Vegetables and Fruits

Add carrots, sweet potatoes, apricots, and prunes. Continue simmering for 30-45 minutes until vegetables are tender.

7

Finish and Serve

Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Garnish with cilantro, mint, and almonds. Serve hot over couscous or rice.
