Lamington

Sponge Cake Squares Coated in Chocolate and Coconut

Easy 60 min 16 pieces Australian

Lamingtons are Australia's beloved afternoon tea treat - light, fluffy sponge cake squares dipped in chocolate icing and rolled in desiccated coconut. These delightful little cakes are perfect with a cup of tea and make a wonderful addition to any celebration or gathering.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Sponge Cake:

  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled

For the Chocolate Icing:

  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1/3 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

For Coating:

  • 2 cups desiccated coconut
  • Extra coconut for garnish

Optional Fillings:

  • 1/2 cup raspberry jam
  • 1/2 cup whipped cream
  • Fresh berries for decoration

Instructions

1

Prepare the Cake

Preheat oven to 350¬∞F (180¬∞C). Grease and line a 9x9 inch square baking pan with parchment paper. Sift together flour, cornstarch, baking powder, and salt.

2

Make the Batter

In a large bowl, beat eggs and sugar with an electric mixer until pale and fluffy, about 5 minutes. Gently fold in the sifted dry ingredients, then add vanilla and melted butter. Pour into prepared pan.

3

Bake the Cake

Bake for 20-25 minutes until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Let the cake cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

4

Cut and Prepare

Once completely cool, cut the cake into 16 equal squares (4x4). Place the squares on a wire rack set over a baking sheet to catch drips.

5

Make the Chocolate Icing

In a medium bowl, whisk together powdered sugar and cocoa powder. Add milk, melted butter, vanilla, and salt. Whisk until smooth and glossy. The icing should be thick but pourable.

6

Coat the Lamingtons

Working with one cake square at a time, dip it into the chocolate icing, ensuring all sides are coated. Allow excess icing to drip off, then roll in desiccated coconut. Place on the wire rack to set.

7

Set and Serve

Let the lamingtons set for at least 1 hour at room temperature. For traditional lamingtons, you can split them and fill with jam and cream. Serve with tea or coffee.
