Feijoada

Black Bean Stew with Pork and Beef, Served with Rice

Medium 180 min 8 servings Brazilian

Feijoada is Brazil's national dish, a hearty black bean stew packed with various cuts of beef. This slow-cooked masterpiece is traditionally served with rice, collard greens, and orange slices, creating a complete and satisfying meal that brings people together.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Base:

  • 2 cups dried black beans (or 3 cans cooked, drained & rinsed)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 carrots, sliced into half-moons
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 200g mushrooms (button or portobello), chopped
  • 2 smoked tofu sausages or smoked tempeh (optional, for extra “meaty” bite)

For the Seasoning:

  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

For Serving:

  • Cooked white rice
  • Collard greens (couve)
  • Orange slices
  • Farofa (toasted cassava flour)
  • Hot sauce (molho de pimenta)

Instructions

1

Prepare the Beans

Drain the soaked beans and place them in a large pot with bay leaves, crushed garlic, and quartered onion. Cover with water and bring to a boil, then simmer for 1 hour until beans are tender.

2

Build the stew

Stir in mushrooms and zucchini, cook until they release liquid and shrink, Add tomato paste, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, and bay leaves. Stir well, Add beans, soy sauce, and vegetable broth, Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer.

3

Sauté the Aromatics

Heat olive oil in a large pot, Add onion, cook until soft (5 minutes), Add garlic, carrots, and bell pepper. Cook 5–6 minutes until slightly softened.

4

Combine and Simmer

Cook uncovered for 30–40 minutes (stir occasionally), Add more water if it gets too thick, Season with salt, pepper, and chili flakes to taste.

5

Adjust Seasoning

Remove bay leaves and onion quarters. Taste and adjust salt and pepper. The stew should be thick and the beans should be very soft.

6

Prepare Accompaniments

While the feijoada simmers, prepare white rice, sauté collard greens with garlic, and slice oranges. Prepare farofa by toasting cassava flour with butter.

7

Serve and Enjoy

Serve the feijoada hot with rice, collard greens, orange slices, farofa, and hot sauce. Let guests help themselves to their preferred accompaniments.

Tips & Variations

🍳 Cooking Tips

  • The smoked paprika + bay leaf + soy sauce combo is what gives this vegan version that “smoky-savory” feijoada feel
  • Like the traditional dish, it tastes even better the next day after flavors meld.
  • Skim off excess fat during cooking for a cleaner taste

🌶️ Flavor Variations

  • Add more smoked meats for deeper flavor
  • Include orange zest in the cooking liquid
  • Use different types of beans for variety

🥗 Traditional Sides

  • Serve with Brazilian-style rice (arroz brasileiro)
  • Include vinaigrette salsa for freshness
  • Add plantain chips for crunch
