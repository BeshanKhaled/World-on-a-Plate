Biryani

Aromatic Rice Dish with Tender Meat and Fragrant Spices

Medium 60 min 6 servings Indian

Biryani is a fragrant rice dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It's made with long-grain basmati rice, tender meat (usually chicken, lamb, or beef), and a blend of aromatic spices including cardamom, cinnamon, and saffron.

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 cardamom pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Meat Marinade:

  • 1 kg chicken/lamb, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

For the Biryani Masala:

  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves
  • 1/2 cup cilantro leaves
  • 4 green chilies, slit
  • 1/2 cup ghee or oil
  • 1/4 cup milk with saffron strands

Instructions

1

Marinate the Meat

Mix all marinade ingredients with the meat and let it marinate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight in the refrigerator.

2

Prepare the Rice

Wash basmati rice until water runs clear. Soak in water for 30 minutes. Boil with spices until 70% cooked, then drain.

3

Cook the Meat

In a large pot, heat ghee and fry onions until golden brown. Add marinated meat and cook until tender and well-browned.

4

Layer the Biryani

In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer half the rice, then the meat mixture, followed by mint, cilantro, and green chilies. Top with remaining rice.

5

Dum Cooking

Drizzle saffron milk over the top layer. Seal the pot with aluminum foil and lid. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes.

6

Rest and Serve

Let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before opening. Gently mix the layers and serve hot with raita and salad.
