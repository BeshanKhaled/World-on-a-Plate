Pavlova

Light Meringue Dessert with Fresh Fruit and Cream

Medium 90 min 8 servings Australian

Pavlova is a light and airy dessert that's a national treasure in Australia and New Zealand. It features a crisp outer shell with a soft, marshmallow-like center, topped with whipped cream and fresh seasonal fruits. The contrast of textures makes it a truly special dessert.

Ingredients

For the Meringue:

  • 6 large egg whites, at room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups (300g) superfine sugar
  • 1 tsp white vinegar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/4 tsp salt

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 2 tbsp powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 cups mixed fresh fruits (berries, kiwi, passion fruit)
  • Fresh mint leaves for garnish

For Serving:

  • Parchment paper
  • Piping bag with large star tip (optional)
  • Cooling rack

Instructions

1

Prepare the Oven

Preheat oven to 300¬∞F (150¬∞C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and draw a 9-inch circle as a guide. Set aside.

2

Beat the Egg Whites

In a clean, dry bowl, beat egg whites with salt until soft peaks form. Gradually add sugar, 1 tablespoon at a time, beating well after each addition.

3

Add Final Ingredients

Continue beating until stiff, glossy peaks form. Gently fold in vinegar, vanilla, and cornstarch until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.

4

Shape the Pavlova

Spoon the meringue onto the prepared baking sheet, using the circle as a guide. Create a slight depression in the center for the topping. Smooth the sides.

5

Bake and Cool

Place in the center of the oven and immediately reduce temperature to 250¬∞F (120¬∞C). Bake for 1 hour, then turn off oven and let cool completely inside.

6

Prepare the Topping

Whip cream with powdered sugar and vanilla until soft peaks form. Refrigerate until ready to use.

7

Assemble and Serve

Carefully transfer the cooled meringue to a serving plate. Fill the center with whipped cream and arrange fresh fruits on top. Garnish with mint leaves and serve immediately.
