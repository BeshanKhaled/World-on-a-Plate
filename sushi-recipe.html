Sushi

Fresh Fish and Rice Wrapped in Nori with Wasabi and Ginger

Hard 45 min 4 servings Japanese

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish featuring vinegared rice combined with various ingredients, typically seafood, vegetables, and occasionally tropical fruits. This recipe focuses on making classic maki rolls with fresh fish and vegetables.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Sushi Rice:

  • 2 cups short-grain Japanese rice
  • 2 1/4 cups water
  • 1/4 cup rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt

For the Filling:

  • 4 sheets nori (dried seaweed)
  • 8 oz fresh salmon or tuna, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Wasabi paste
  • Pickled ginger

For Serving:

  • Soy sauce
  • Wasabi paste
  • Pickled ginger
  • Bamboo rolling mat

Instructions

1

Prepare the Rice

Rinse rice until water runs clear. Cook rice with water according to package instructions. Mix vinegar, sugar, and salt, then fold into hot rice. Let cool to room temperature.

2

Prepare the Nori

Place a sheet of nori on the bamboo mat, shiny side down. Wet your hands with water to prevent sticking.

3

Spread the Rice

Spread about 3/4 cup of rice evenly over the nori, leaving a 1-inch strip empty at the top edge.

4

Add the Filling

Place fish, cucumber, avocado, and green onions in a line across the center of the rice. Add a small amount of wasabi if desired.

5

Roll the Sushi

Lift the bottom edge of the mat and roll tightly away from you, pressing gently to form a compact roll. Wet the empty nori edge to seal.

6

Cut and Serve

Wet a sharp knife and cut the roll into 6-8 pieces. Serve immediately with soy sauce, wasabi, and pickled ginger.
