BBQ Ribs

Slow-Smoked Pork Ribs with Tangy Barbecue Sauce

Hard 240 min 4 servings American

BBQ Ribs are a quintessential American barbecue dish, featuring tender, fall-off-the-bone pork ribs that have been slow-cooked with a dry rub and finished with a sticky, tangy barbecue sauce. The key is low and slow cooking to achieve perfect tenderness.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Ribs:

  • 2 beef ribs (about 4-5 lbs total)
  • 2 tbsp yellow mustard
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar

For the Dry Rub:

  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 2 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp dried oregano

For the Barbecue Sauce:

  • 1 cup ketchup
  • 1/2 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp molasses
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

For Smoking:

  • Wood chips (hickory, apple, or cherry)
  • Aluminum foil
  • Spray bottle with apple juice

Instructions

1

Prepare the Ribs

Remove the membrane from the back of the ribs. Rinse and pat dry. Apply a thin layer of mustard and vinegar to help the rub stick.

2

Apply the Dry Rub

Mix all dry rub ingredients in a bowl. Generously coat both sides of the ribs with the rub, pressing it into the meat. Let rest for 1 hour at room temperature.

3

Prepare the Smoker

Set up your smoker or grill for indirect cooking at 225-250¬∞F (107-121¬∞C). Add wood chips for smoke flavor. Place a water pan in the smoker for moisture.

4

Smoke the Ribs

Place ribs on the smoker, meat side up. Smoke for 2 hours, maintaining temperature and adding wood chips as needed. Spritz with apple juice every 30 minutes.

5

Wrap and Continue Cooking

After 2 hours, wrap ribs in aluminum foil with a splash of apple juice. Return to smoker and cook for 1-2 more hours until meat is tender and pulls away from bone.

6

Make the Barbecue Sauce

While ribs are cooking, combine all sauce ingredients in a saucepan. Simmer over low heat for 20-30 minutes until thickened, stirring occasionally.

7

Glaze and Serve

Remove ribs from foil and brush with barbecue sauce. Return to smoker for 15-20 minutes to set the glaze. Let rest for 10 minutes before cutting and serving.
