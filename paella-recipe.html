Paella

Valencian Rice Dish with Seafood, Chicken, and Saffron

Medium 45 min 6 servings Spanish

Paella is a traditional Spanish rice dish that originated in Valencia. It's characterized by its distinctive yellow color from saffron and its socarrat - the crispy, caramelized rice at the bottom. This version combines seafood, chicken, and vegetables for a complete meal.

Ingredients

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups short-grain rice (Bomba or Arborio)
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 1/4 tsp saffron threads
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tomato, grated

For the Protein:

  • 4 chicken thighs, cut into pieces
  • 12 large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 12 mussels, cleaned
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup green beans, trimmed

For Seasoning:

  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions

1

Prepare the Stock

Heat chicken stock in a saucepan and add saffron threads. Let steep for 10 minutes while you prepare other ingredients.

2

Cook the Chicken

Heat olive oil in a paella pan or large skillet. Season chicken with salt and pepper, then brown on all sides until golden. Remove and set aside.

3

Sauté the Vegetables

In the same pan, sauté onion and garlic until softened. Add bell pepper and grated tomato, cook for 2-3 minutes.

4

Add Rice and Spices

Stir in rice and smoked paprika, coating the rice with oil. Cook for 1-2 minutes until rice becomes translucent.

5

Simmer the Paella

Pour in hot saffron stock and return chicken to the pan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes without stirring.

6

Add Seafood and Vegetables

Arrange shrimp, mussels, peas, and green beans on top. Continue cooking for 5-7 minutes until rice is tender and seafood is cooked.

7

Create Socarrat

Increase heat to medium-high for the last 2-3 minutes to create the crispy bottom layer. Remove from heat and let rest for 5 minutes.

8

Serve

Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges. Enjoy the paella directly from the pan for the authentic experience.

Tips & Variations

🍳 Cooking Tips

  • Use a wide, shallow pan for even cooking
  • Don't stir the rice after adding stock - this creates the socarrat
  • Let the paella rest for 5 minutes before serving

🌊 Seafood Options

  • Substitute with clams, squid, or fish fillets
  • Use frozen seafood if fresh isn't available
  • Add lobster or crab for a special occasion

🥘 Pan Alternatives

  • Use a large cast-iron skillet if you don't have a paella pan
  • Ensure the pan is wide enough for even heat distribution
  • Don't overcrowd the pan
