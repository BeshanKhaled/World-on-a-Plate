Bobotie

Spiced Minced Meat Baked with an Egg-Based Topping

Medium 75 min 6 servings South African

Bobotie is South Africa's national dish, a flavorful minced meat casserole with aromatic spices, dried fruits, and a golden egg custard topping. This Cape Malay-inspired dish perfectly balances sweet, savory, and spicy flavors in every bite.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Meat Mixture:

  • 1 kg ground beef or lamb
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 slices white bread, soaked in milk
  • 1/4 cup milk

For the Spices:

  • 2 tbsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper

For the Flavorings:

  • 1/4 cup raisins or sultanas
  • 1/4 cup dried apricots, chopped
  • 2 tbsp chutney (mango or apricot)
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice

For the Egg Topping:

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper
  • 2 bay leaves for garnish

Instructions

1

Prepare the Bread

Soak the bread slices in milk until soft, then squeeze out excess milk and set aside.

2

Cook the Onions

Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add chopped onions and cook until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook for 1 more minute.

3

Add the Meat

Add the ground meat to the pan and cook, breaking it up with a wooden spoon, until browned and cooked through, about 8-10 minutes.

4

Season and Flavor

Add all the spices, soaked bread, dried fruits, chutney, Worcestershire sauce, brown sugar, and lemon juice. Stir well and cook for 5 minutes to blend flavors.

5

Transfer to Baking Dish

Transfer the meat mixture to a greased 9x13 inch baking dish. Press down gently to create an even surface.

6

Prepare Egg Topping

In a bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, salt, and white pepper. Pour over the meat mixture and place bay leaves on top for garnish.

7

Bake to Perfection

Bake in a preheated 350°F (180°C) oven for 30-35 minutes until the egg topping is set and golden brown. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.

Tips & Variations

🍳 Cooking Tips

  • Use a combination of beef and lamb for the best flavor
  • Don't skip soaking the bread - it helps bind the mixture
  • Let the dish rest after baking for easier slicing

🌶️ Spice Variations

  • Adjust curry powder to your taste preference
  • Add more cinnamon for a sweeter profile
  • Include fresh ginger for extra warmth

🥗 Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with yellow rice and chutney
  • Accompany with a fresh green salad
  • Garnish with fresh cilantro or parsley
