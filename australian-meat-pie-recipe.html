Australian Meat Pie

Flaky Pastry Filled with Savory Minced Beef and Rich Gravy

Medium 90 min 6 servings Australian

The Australian Meat Pie is a beloved national dish that's perfect for any occasion. This savory pie features a flaky, buttery pastry filled with tender minced beef in a rich, flavorful gravy. Often enjoyed at sporting events, family gatherings, or as a comforting meal, this pie represents the heart of Australian comfort food.

Ingredients

For the Pastry:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup cold butter, cubed
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

For the Filling:

  • 1 lb ground beef (mince)
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 cup beef stock
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

For Serving:

  • Tomato sauce (ketchup)
  • Mashed potatoes
  • Green peas

Instructions

1

Make the Pastry

In a large bowl, mix flour and salt. Cut in cold butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Gradually add cold water, mixing until dough forms. Wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.

2

Prepare the Filling

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute more.

3

Cook the Beef

Add ground beef to the skillet and cook until browned, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Drain excess fat if necessary.

4

Add Seasonings

Stir in tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, thyme, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for 2 minutes to blend flavors.

5

Thicken the Gravy

Add beef stock and bring to a simmer. Mix cornstarch with 2 tbsp water and stir into the mixture. Cook until thickened, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool completely.

6

Assemble the Pies

Preheat oven to 400¬∞F (200¬∞C). Divide pastry in half. Roll out one half and cut into 6 circles (about 6 inches each). Place in greased muffin tins or pie molds.

7

Fill and Top

Fill each pastry case with the cooled beef mixture. Roll out remaining pastry and cut 6 circles for tops. Place over filling and crimp edges. Cut a small slit in the top of each pie.

8

Bake and Serve

Brush tops with beaten egg and bake for 25-30 minutes until golden brown. Let cool for 10 minutes before serving with tomato sauce, mashed potatoes, and peas.
