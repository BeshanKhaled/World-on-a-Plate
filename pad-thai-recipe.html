Pad Thai

Thailand's Most Famous Noodle Dish

Easy 30 min 4 servings Thai

Pad Thai is a stir-fried rice noodle dish that's a perfect balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors. This iconic Thai street food features rice noodles, eggs, tofu, shrimp, and bean sprouts.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Sauce:

  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 tsp chili powder

For the Noodles:

  • 8 oz (225g) rice noodles
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 8 oz (225g) shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 8 oz (225g) firm tofu, cubed
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions

1

Prepare the Noodles

Soak rice noodles in warm water for 30 minutes until they're flexible but still firm. Drain and set aside.

2

Make the Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together tamarind paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, vinegar, and chili powder until well combined.

3

Stir-Fry the Base

Heat oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat. Add garlic and shallots, stir-fry for 1 minute until fragrant.

4

Cook the Protein

Push the aromatics to one side and add beaten eggs. Scramble until just set, then add shrimp and tofu. Cook until shrimp turns pink.

5

Add Noodles and Sauce

Add the soaked noodles and pour in the sauce. Toss everything together until noodles are well coated and heated through.

6

Finish with Vegetables

Add bean sprouts and green onions, toss for 1 minute until sprouts are slightly wilted but still crisp.

7

Serve and Garnish

Transfer to serving plates and garnish with crushed peanuts, cilantro, and lime wedges. Serve immediately while hot.
