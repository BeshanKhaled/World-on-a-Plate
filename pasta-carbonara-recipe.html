Pasta Carbonara

Classic Roman Pasta with Eggs, Cheese, and Pancetta

Easy 25 min 4 servings Italian

Pasta Carbonara is a classic Italian dish from Rome, featuring spaghetti tossed with a creamy sauce made from eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and crispy pancetta or guanciale. The key is to create a silky sauce that coats the pasta without scrambling the eggs.

Ingredients

For the Pasta:

  • 1 lb (450g) spaghetti
  • 1 tbsp salt for pasta water
  • 1/2 cup reserved pasta water

For the Sauce:

  • 4 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1 cup Pecorino Romano cheese, freshly grated
  • 1/2 cup Parmigiano-Reggiano, freshly grated
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper, freshly ground
  • 1/4 tsp salt

For the Pancetta:

  • 8 oz (225g) pancetta or guanciale, cubed
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

For Garnish:

  • Extra Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)
  • Additional black pepper

Instructions

1

Prepare the Sauce Base

In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, grated cheeses, black pepper, and salt until well combined. Set aside at room temperature.

2

Cook the Pancetta

In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add pancetta and cook until crispy and golden brown, about 8-10 minutes. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

3

Boil the Pasta

Bring a large pot of salted water to boil. Cook spaghetti according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.

4

Combine Pasta and Sauce

Add hot pasta to the skillet with pancetta. Remove from heat and quickly toss to combine. The residual heat will prevent the eggs from scrambling.

5

Add the Egg Mixture

Working quickly, add the egg mixture to the pasta, tossing constantly. The hot pasta will cook the eggs into a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed for consistency.

6

Serve Immediately

Transfer to serving plates and garnish with extra cheese and black pepper. Serve immediately while hot and creamy.
