Coq au Vin

Braised Chicken in Red Wine with Mushrooms and Pearl Onions

Medium 90 min 6 servings French

Coq au Vin is a classic French dish that transforms humble chicken into an elegant, wine-braised masterpiece. The chicken is marinated in red wine, then slowly cooked with mushrooms, pearl onions, and bacon until tender and flavorful.

Share this recipe:

Ingredients

For the Marinade:

  • 1 bottle (750ml) red wine (Burgundy or Pinot Noir)
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns

For the Chicken:

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into 8 pieces
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 4 oz (115g) bacon or lardons, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

For the Vegetables:

  • 8 oz (225g) button mushrooms, halved
  • 12 pearl onions, peeled
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup chicken stock

For Garnish:

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Cooked egg noodles or mashed potatoes

Instructions

1

Marinate the Chicken

Combine wine, carrots, onion, garlic, bay leaves, thyme, and peppercorns in a large bowl. Add chicken pieces and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.

2

Prepare the Chicken

Remove chicken from marinade and pat dry. Strain and reserve the marinade liquid. Season chicken with salt and pepper, then dust with flour.

3

Brown the Chicken

In a large Dutch oven, cook bacon until crispy. Remove bacon and set aside. Add olive oil and brown chicken pieces on all sides until golden. Work in batches to avoid overcrowding.

4

Cook the Vegetables

Add mushrooms and pearl onions to the pot. Cook until mushrooms release their liquid and onions begin to soften, about 5 minutes. Add garlic and tomato paste, cook for 1 minute.

5

Braise the Chicken

Return chicken and bacon to the pot. Pour in reserved marinade liquid and chicken stock. Bring to a simmer, then cover and cook on low heat for 45-60 minutes until chicken is tender.

6

Finish and Serve

Uncover and simmer for 10-15 minutes to reduce the sauce. Season to taste with salt and pepper. Garnish with parsley and serve hot over egg noodles or mashed potatoes.
